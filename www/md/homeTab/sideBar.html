AdaptWest Climate Resilience Data Explorer


Welcome to the AdaptWest Climate Resilience Data Explorer. AdaptWest is a spatial database designed to help land management agencies and other organizations implement strategies that promote resilience, protect biodiversity, and conserve and enhance the adaptation potential of natural systems in the face of a changing climate.

The goals of this Climate Resilience Data Explorer are to:

  • Familiarize users with climate resilience concepts;
  • Explore information resources that can help conservation practitioners enhance climate resilience and connectivity and address climate change threats in the context of regional planning;
  • Support conservation management that increases the adaptive capacity of the landscape and its ability to support native species and ecosystems into the future by protecting areas which play a key role in facilitating climate adaptation and mitigation.

This viewer was developed through collaboration between:
  • Justin Beckers (Canadian Forest Service, Natural Resources Canada)
  • Carlos Carroll (Klamath Center for Conservation Research)
All data displayed in this tool are freely-available at http:/adaptwest.databasin.org.
